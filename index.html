Commonality

We live in a world that is more both more connected and more isolated than ever. Real-life friendships are harder to come by, and it’s not uncommon to feel like no one around you shares your interests. With the slow disappearance of “third spaces” like coffee shops, clubs, and community centers, plus the rise of screen-heavy, isolated lifestyles, it’s easy to lose touch — not just with people, but with the hobbies and passions that make us feel like ourselves.

That’s where our app comes in. We believe no one should have to give up what they love just because they don’t know someone else who loves it too. Our platform makes it easy to find people who get it — whether it’s rock climbing, board games, tennis, cooking, or anything else. Just set up a profile, add your interests, and we’ll connect you with other people who share your interests. Simple, human, and built for genuine connection.

Technologies Used:

HTML, Tailwind CSS, JS, TS, React, React Router 7, Express, PostgreSQL, Docker, Insomnia, Zod, Git/GitHub, Mailgun, REST APIs, Miro, DBDiagram

Etern.life Memorial Map

I developed Etern.life when I was the Marketing Manager at Passages International, Inc. We had hired a few third-party developers who did not manage to bring this project to life, so I brought the project in-house and built it myself. For this project, I created a WordPress website and used a heavily edited business directory plugin to create an ash scattering memorial map.

This website lets users mark the location that they scattered a loved one's ashes and create an online memorial. The location is pinned on a map, and anyone can see the memorial and where this person was scattered.

We synced access codes to an Oracle NetSuite database with an API for purchase validation.

Etern.life is currently live, and has hundreds of memorials across 6 continents.

Technologies Used:

WordPress, HTML, CSS, PHP, and a variety of customized WordPress plugins.

Turning Pointe Health & Wellness

Turning Pointe Health & Wellness was a medical clinic offering a combination of Eastern and Western medicine. After being hired on to create the website for this startup, I quickly learned that the company was missing a brand identity, a complete grasp of its offerings, and messaging. A major part of this project became a marketing/branding consulting project to decide what would go on the website.

This website was built using WordPress. It's a relatively simple design, but it highlights the branding and content creation work that I did for the company.

Unfortunately, due to a falling out between the founders, this company did not make it, but I'm working on putting a version of the website up here as part of my portfolio.

Technologies Used:

WordPress, HTML, CSS, PHP, and a variety of WordPress plugins.
